Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure

What is the problem?

Unlike us in a highly-developed country, there are a lot of people in the world who don't have the luxuries we have.

Examples include:

  • Cell phones
  • Cars
  • Public transportation
  • Electricity

Including the above, there are an inifnite number of things that could be listed that people in third-world countries don't have that we have.

Imagine

How would you react if you saw this but with no lights or building? Think about what your life would be like. What if you weren't in the vehicle travelling or that bus you pass doesn't exist.

Would you want your life to be like that?

Why should we care?

Here are some important facts that could relate to everyday life here is:

About 2.6 billion people in the developing world can't access electricity 24/7.

1 to 1.5 billion people don't have access to reliable phone services.

2.5 billion people worldwide lack access to basic sanitation and almost 800 million people lack access to water.

Imagine what our lives would be like if we didn't have any of the things listed above. Just for reference, here are just a small number of luxuries that we wouldn't have as a result of the above.

Netflix

The Internet

Cars

Shelters

Clean Water

Electricity

Phone Data

Clean, Healthy Food

Life would be terrible without all of these things, wouldn't it!

How You Could Help

UNIDO

UNIDO, the United Nations Industrial Development Organization, is a branch of the UN (United Nations) that is focused on the development of industrial work in third-world countries. They have numerous projects throughout the world.
